Letters from Whitechapel

The night is yours. You move unseen through the gaslit streets of Whitechapel, evading the Metropolitan Police as they close in. Reach your hideout before they find you — every move counts, and the net grows tighter with each round.

How to play
1

Your goal: Move Jack from his starting position to his rose-ringed hideout before the turn limit expires. Click any orange-ringed node to move there — your position glows green. A purple zone on the board shows the approximate area where the hideout lies.

2

Two kinds of node: Large circular nodes are Jack nodes — the positions you move between. Small squares are cop nodes — the intersections where police stand and act. Each alley between two Jack nodes passes through one or more cop nodes.

3

Police turn: After you move, each cop moves up to 2 steps along the police network, then chooses one action. Search — they check all Jack nodes adjacent to their position against your trail; confirmed nodes turn blue permanently. Arrest — they attempt to catch you on those same adjacent nodes. If you are there, the police win. All cops share every discovery instantly.

Important details

• Cops do not block your movement — you can walk through any alley regardless of where cops stand.

• When a cop arrests, they target all adjacent Jack nodes simultaneously, not just one.

• A failed arrest or missed search still tells the police something — they now know you were not on those nodes at that moment.

• The purple-shaded zone on the board marks the approximate area where your hideout lies — the police know this zone at the start of the game.
